<p><p>FR PP compounds, or flame-retardant polypropylene compounds, are categorized into two main market types: halogen type and halogen-free type. Halogen type compounds incorporate halogen elements, such as bromine or chlorine, to enhance flame resistance. However, they may release toxic emissions when burned. In contrast, halogen-free type compounds use alternative materials like phosphorus or nitrogen, offering a safer environmental profile with reduced toxic emissions. Both types are employed in various applications, including automotive, electronics, and construction, to ensure safety and compliance with fire regulations.</p></p>
