<p><ul><li>Aerobic Digestion Waste Management</li><li>Anaerobic Digestion Waste Management</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Commercial food waste disposal systems primarily include aerobic and anaerobic digestion waste management. Aerobic digestion employs oxygen to break down organic material, converting it into compost and biogas, which can be used for energy. This method is generally faster and produces a nutrient-rich end product. Conversely, anaerobic digestion operates in low-oxygen environments, generating biogas and digestate, a rich fertilizer. This method is effective for larger volumes and produces renewable energy while significantly reducing waste volume. Both systems contribute to sustainable waste management practices.</p></p>
