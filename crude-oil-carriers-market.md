<p><p>The crude oil carriers market consists of various vessel types based on size and capacity. VLCCs (Very Large Crude Carriers) and ULCCs (Ultra Large Crude Carriers) typically transport larger volumes, over 200,000 deadweight tons (DWT), making them key players in long-haul trade. Suezmax vessels are designed to transit the Suez Canal, with capacities around 120,000 DWT. Aframax tankers, ranging between 80,000 to 120,000 DWT, cater to medium-haul routes, while Panamax vessels, up to 80,000 DWT, fit the Panama Canal dimensions for flexibility in trade.</p></p>
