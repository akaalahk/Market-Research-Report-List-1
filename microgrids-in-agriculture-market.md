<p><p>Microgrids in agriculture can be categorized into three main types: AC Microgrid Systems, which utilize alternating current and are ideal for connecting with existing power grids; DC Microgrid Systems, designed for direct current applications, often preferred for renewable energy sources like solar panels; and Hybrid Microgrid Systems, which combine both AC and DC components, allowing for flexibility in energy management. These systems enhance energy efficiency, reduce costs, and support sustainable farming practices by integrating renewable energy sources.</p></p>
