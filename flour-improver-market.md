<p><p>Flour improvers are additives that enhance the quality and performance of flour in baking. The market is segmented into three types: powder, liquid, and granule. Powdered improvers are commonly used for their convenience and long shelf life, while liquid formats offer easier mixing with dough and can provide specific benefits like moisture retention. Granular improvers combine characteristics of both powder and liquid, allowing for precise dosing and improved handling during bulk processing. Each type serves distinct baking requirements.</p></p>
