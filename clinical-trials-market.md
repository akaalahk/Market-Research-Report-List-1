<p><ul><li>Phase I</li><li>Phase II</li><li>Phase III</li><li>Phase IV</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Clinical trials are categorized into four phases, each serving a specific purpose in drug development. Phase I trials focus on safety and dosage in a small group of healthy volunteers. Phase II trials assess efficacy and further evaluate safety in a larger patient group. Phase III trials involve larger populations to confirm effectiveness, monitor side effects, and compare with standard treatments. Phase IV trials, conducted after market approval, gather additional information on long-term effects and overall effectiveness in diverse populations.</p></p>

<p><p>The clinical trials market encompasses various applications including interventional, observational, and expanded access trials.
